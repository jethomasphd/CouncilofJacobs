The Science

This experiment sits at the intersection of several well-documented phenomena. None of them are speculative. All of them are measurable.

1 — Implicit Egotism & Name-Letter Effect

Decades of research in social psychology demonstrate that people are disproportionately drawn to others who share their name, initials, or even name-letters. Pelham, Mirenberg & Jones (2002) showed this at population scale — people named Louis are overrepresented in St. Louis; people named Georgia cluster in Georgia. The name is not just a label. It is a gravitational field.

2 — Minimal Group Paradigm

Tajfel's classic experiments proved that humans will form in-group loyalty based on criteria as trivial as coin-flip assignment. A shared name is orders of magnitude more salient than a coin flip. It carries history, phonetics, cultural resonance. The Council of Jacobs is a maximal minimal group — the thinnest possible thread of shared identity, stretched across maximum human diversity.

3 — Collective Intelligence Amplification

Woolley et al. (2010) demonstrated that group intelligence is not predicted by the average IQ of members, but by social sensitivity, conversational turn-taking, and demographic diversity. A global network of Jacob Thomases — spanning continents, professions, cultures, and languages — is diversity-maximized by design. The name holds the center. The diversity does the work.

4 — The Refraction Hypothesis

This is the novel contribution. In optics, refraction occurs when light passes through a medium of different density and its path bends. In the Council, human intelligence passes through the medium of AI and refracts into something no single mind could produce. Hundreds of Jacob Thomases contribute raw perspectives — bullet points, brainstorms, lived experience. A custom-built JacobThomasGPT aggregates, synthesizes, and delivers a report that is not AI-generated in the hollow sense. It is AI-refracted human intelligence. The human signal, bent through a new lens, arriving somewhere none of them could have reached alone.
